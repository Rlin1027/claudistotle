# Citation Guide for Humanities & Philosophy

## Quick Format Reference

### APA 7th Edition

**Journal article:**

```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Periodical, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx
```

**Book:**

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle. Publisher.
```

**Book chapter:**

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), Title of book (pp. xx-xx). Publisher.
```

**In-text:** `(Author, Year, p. X)` or `Author (Year) argues that...`

---

### Chicago Manual of Style (17th Ed.) — Notes-Bibliography

Preferred in many humanities and philosophy journals.

**First citation (footnote):**

```
1. First Last, Title of Book (Place: Publisher, Year), page.
2. First Last, "Article Title," Journal Name vol, no. issue (Year): pages.
```

**Subsequent citations:**

```
3. Last, Short Title, page.
```

**Bibliography entry:**

```
Last, First. Title of Book. Place: Publisher, Year.
Last, First. "Article Title." Journal Name vol, no. issue (Year): pages.
```

---

### MLA 9th Edition

**Works Cited entry:**

```
Last, First. Title of Source. Title of Container, Other contributors, Version, Number, Publisher, Publication date, Location.
```

**In-text:** `(Author page)` — no comma, no "p."
Example: `(Nussbaum 42)`

## Philosophy-Specific Citation Conventions

### Classical Texts (Standardized References)

Classical philosophical texts use standardized reference systems, NOT page numbers from a specific edition:

| Author | Standard Reference | Example |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **Plato** | Stephanus numbers | *Republic* 514a-520a |
| **Aristotle** | Bekker numbers | *Nicomachean Ethics* 1094a1-5 |
| **Kant** | Academy edition (Ak.) | *Critique of Pure Reason* A51/B75 (A/B edition) |
| **Hegel** | Paragraph numbers (§) | *Philosophy of Right* §257 |
| **Wittgenstein** | Section numbers | *Philosophical Investigations* §66 |
| **Aquinas** | Part, Question, Article | *Summa Theologiae* I, q.2, a.3 |
| **Descartes** | Adam & Tannery (AT) | AT VII, 25 |
| **Heidegger** | Gesamtausgabe (GA) | *Being and Time* GA 2, p. 42 / SZ 42 |

**Format**: Always cite the standardized reference, then optionally the translation used:

```
Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics, 1094a1-5. (Trans. Ross, revised Ackrill & Urmson, Oxford, 1998)
```

### Chinese Classical Texts

| Text | Standard Reference | Example |
|------|-------------------|---------|
| **Analects (Lunyu)** | Book.Chapter | Lunyu, Learning First, 1.1 |
| **Mencius (Mengzi)** | Book.Section | Mengzi, King Hui of Liang (Part A), 1A.1 |
| **Dao De Jing** | Chapter | Dao De Jing, Chapter 1 |
| **Zhuangzi** | Chapter name | Zhuangzi, Free Wandering (Xiaoyao You) |
| **Great Learning (Da Xue)** | Section | Da Xue, Essential Teachings, Chapter 1 |
| **Doctrine of the Mean (Zhongyong)** | Chapter | Zhongyong, Chapter 1 |

**Format with translation:**

```
Analects, Governance (Wei Zheng), 2.4: "At fifteen I set my heart on learning." (Trans. Ames & Rosemont, 1998, p. 76)
```

## Citation Integration Patterns

### Signal Phrase Patterns (Engaging with Sources)

**Presenting a view:**

- "According to Rawls (1971), justice requires..."
- "As Nussbaum (2006, p. 78) argues,..."
- "Butler (1990) theorizes gender as..."

**Agreeing/Building:**

- "Following Heidegger's analysis of Dasein (SZ §12),..."
- "Extending MacIntyre's (1981) insight that..."

**Disagreeing:**

- "Contra Singer (1975), I argue that..."
- "While Searle's (1980) Chinese Room argument is influential, it fails to..."
- "Pace Derrida ($1967, p. 158$), I contend that..."

**Synthesizing:**

- "Both Taylor (1989) and MacIntyre (1981) agree that... However, they differ on..."
- "While the analytic tradition (Williamson, 2007) treats X as..., the continental tradition (Heidegger, SZ §44) understands it as..."

### Avoid These Citation Errors

| Error | Example | Fix |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **Drive-by citation** | "This is important (Smith 2019)." | Explain HOW Smith supports your claim |
| **Citation dump** | "(A 2019; B 2020; C 2021; D 2022)" | Cite individually with engagement, or use "See, among others," |
| **Ghost citation** | Citing a work you haven't read | Read it, or cite the secondary source explicitly: "as cited in..." |
| **Over-reliance on secondary** | Always citing "According to SEP..." | Go to the primary source |
| **Inconsistent format** | Mixing APA and Chicago randomly | Pick one format and apply consistently |
